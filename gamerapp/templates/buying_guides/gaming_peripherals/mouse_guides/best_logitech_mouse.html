The Logitech G502 X is the brand's best pointer thus far. However, this is due to more than simply the 25K sensor and the comfy design. Between the wired and wireless models, everyone may enjoy the moderate slopes and number of buttons available. The bulk of gamers will prefer the less expensive wired model. Coming in at $79.99 / £79.99, this is still a fairly costly product, but it is a far cry from the $149.99 / £149.99 price of the wireless Plus version.(prices may be higher or lower due to price fluctuations)

Whatever you choose, you'll get the iconic chunky G502 stature with some distinctive RGB effects tossed in. We found button placements and access to be ideal for both shooters and MOBA-style games, with plenty of customizing options around the sniper button.

The X version retains many of the features that contributed to the original G502's popularity. First, the wireless variant is compatible with the PowerPlay gaming mouse pad, which is ideal for individuals who forget to plug in at the end of a session. You also have a swappable scroll wheel. When navigating web pages, standard notched scrolling can be altered to an unlimited free-spin before returning to get more control in gameplay.

In summary, the G502 X is not only Logitech's newest gaming mouse, but also the finest for the majority of players. That's due to its ergonomic form size, simple customisation method, and versatile variety of additional buttons. With some nice RGB and a good battery life in the wireless form, this is a great choice for anyone looking for their first Logitech pointer.

What if you want more out of a wireless Logitech gaming mouse? When it debuted in 2021, the G Pro X Superlight aimed squarely at the ultralight FPS-first category, and it landed with a 63g pointer that boasted exceptional battery life, a flingable compact factor, and a robust build. The additional price will keep the G Pro X Superlight for those who truly need it: esports competitors who require precise precision and rapid movement at the same time. However, we've seen that MSRP drop slightly in recent purchases, to the point where casual FPS players can get a look in.

The Superlight foregoes unnecessary features such as RGB in favor of a lengthy battery life and a highly lightweight physical factor. Logitech is focusing on tournament-oriented gamers here, which is evident throughout the design. However, the internals surely contribute to your agility on the battlefield.

When the G Pro X Superlight was tested, there was a quick pair of clickers with good response times and a reliable, lightning-fast sensor, which enhanced our aim. The lengthy battery life fits nicely into our daily lives, allowing a full day of work to easily transition into an evening of enjoyment without the need to recharge. Actually, using the Superlight for that long period of time was also pleasant, given to the noticeable dome hump and well-placed side buttons.

Yes, the G502 X Plus is a wireless pointer that is priced higher than the Logitech G502 Lightspeed, but if all you want is a cordless existence, the older model is much better value for money nowadays. We frequently see this Logitech gaming mouse on sale for well under $100 / £100, and with the 25K Hero sensor and PowerPlay charging mat compatibility, there's certainly something to get thrilled about here. Our original assessment compared the previous iteration to an older 16K DPI sensor. Since then, it's been upgraded to the 25K version found in the X model, which provides even greater tracking.

The G502's design is often regarded as providing superior ergonomic comfort. The thumb rest on the left side, mild slope hump, and somewhat asymmetrical balance maintain everything calm and easy to manoeuvre. That physical factor has lasted the test of time, thanks in large part to its capacity to store 11 programmable buttons while still allowing you to reach each one in a timely manner. We did find the sniper button to be a little too easy to press, occasionally resulting in an inadvertent strike.

The secret weapon of the Logitech G502 Lightspeed is its weight. It's not a lightweight gaming mouse, but if you prefer to customize your experience, this is one of the few pointers on the market that includes additional weights. This feature has been removed in the newer G502 X, however it is quite useful if you lose control of the pointer during more hectic gameplay situations.

Generally, the G502 Lightspeed is the best wireless Logitech gaming mouse due to its current price point and additional capabilities that appear to have been overlooked in recent models. This is our go-to all-in-one wireless pointer, with an up-to-date sensor, tons of customization choices, and a good battery life (supported by a wirelessly charging mouse pad).

If you don't want to spend in range $40 on a Logitech gaming mouse, the G203 Lightsync is the option for you. Not only is this still capable pointer reasonably priced, but we frequently see it on sale for about $20 or less. Given the high quality and six programmable buttons, that's incredible value.

You're not sacrificing comfort here either (well, not unless you have larger hands). While this is a smaller pointer with a less noticeable hump than we were used to throughout our testing, it remains comfortable over extended hours of play. Furthermore, the G203's smaller form allows it to remain lightweight and easily manoeuvrable, attributes that command a premium in the high-end market.

Yes, the build quality is straightforward, with an all-plastic body and no grip textures. However, you will still discover programmable RGB lights along the body's edges and in the G logo on the rear.

The performance significantly exceeds its pricing. While it isn't a laser-fast high-tech clicker, the sensor under the hood provides reliable tracking. It may be inexpensive, but the G203 can surely keep up. DPI shifting is available in 50 increments, with a maximum of 8K. You may be concerned about making a sacrifice with Logitech's 25K offerings, but the great majority of players never require more than 3,500 dots per inch.

The Logitech G203 provides all of the basic functions of gaming mice ten times its price. By making judicious tradeoffs and retaining everything in a lightweight chassis, it's the best Logitech gaming mouse for those on a tight budget. If you want an inexpensive gaming mouse for larger hands, we recommend the G502 Hero or the Hyperion Fury.

Whether portability is the goal, or you're a fellow small-handed player seeking for a pointer you can actually scale to reach, the Logitech G705 delivers a powerful punch.

The squat shape makes this suitable for those who do not want access to a plethora of extra buttons, but it also provides for greater control and precision when pressing the buttons that are present. There aren't many left-handed gaming mouse models available right now, but Logitech goes above and above to accommodate lefties. This roster includes dozens of ambidextrous and particularly built pointers, including the ever-popular G903, but the Logitech G Pro Wireless takes the cake.

That's because the design is truly ambidextrous. A flat body sacrifices some of the ergonomic comfort that a slightly slanted design provides, but there is still a noticeable hump in the back that keeps you comfy. However, the swappable side buttons secured this position. Magnetically attachable clickers (available in two heights) allow you to quickly swap between right and left-handed setups. This means no superfluous buttons or unintentional clicks.

Aside from the extra clickers, you'll still get a comfortable pointer for both left and right handed use. The matte plastic chassis feels pleasant to the touch, and despite the lack of additional grips on the sides. However, after a longer session, such a material begins to feel slightly clammy.

This is an older equipment, and its age is most visible in the plastic cable. Nonetheless, there's a strong connection here, with extra supports on either side of the socket to keep things safe when charging or playing in wired mode. However, the G Pro's low click latency and lightweight form size (combined with that 25K sensor) help to keep it not only current but also thriving in the modern era.
