The Razer DeathAdder V3 was dubbed a "FPS mouse for those who detest FPS mice" by us. This is due to the fact that it's a very light pointer that isn't just able to lose weight by constricting everything to a small form factor.

The design is Extremely light gaming mice, such as the MSI Clutch GM31 and the Razer Viper V2 Pro mentioned above, are frequently very compact, which makes them uncomfortable for users with larger hands. But the Razer DeathAdder V3 still manages to provide the hefty curves necessary for long-term comfort in a 59g package. Not everyone will be able to use that form; in fact, it is significantly more suited to people with larger hands. It's a quick pointer, though, and stays clear of the traps that many thin gaming mouse have.

It's features Compared to the similarly priced Razer Basilisk V3 model, the lower price does mean you're losing out on RGB support, and there are fewer programmable buttons here, but there are still plenty of great features.

We suggest taking a look at the new Razer Viper V3 Hyperspeed if you like the DeathAdder's design but would prefer a wireless connection. It has a 2.4GHz Hyperspeed connection, is priced the same, and maintains the bigger dome form of the DeathAdder series as opposed to the more conventionally flat Viper range.

Under the hood, you have Razer's newest generation of sensors and switches in addition to the blazingly quick performance one would anticipate from such parts. However, the perks of that low weight were somewhat compromised here by the chunkier form factor, particularly if you're using smaller hands. Nevertheless, in our tests, that sensor was able to handle everything from Apex Legends to The Witcher 3.

Concluding all this although the Razer DeathAdder V3 is a lightweight gaming mouse that appeals to a far larger market than normal, it won't be for everyone. Larger hands are no longer an obstacle in this race, and it has the internal specifications to support that claim as well.

✔ You prefer a smooth grip: Unlike grippy tape or rubber textures that can be bothersome for some users, the soft-touch finish of this mouse offers a comfortable hold without slipping

Now on the market, the most adaptable Razer mouse is the Razer Naga V2 Pro, thanks to its three interchangeable control panels, ergonomic design, and quick internals.

It's design from an angle other than only the side, the Nava V2 Pro stands out from other gaming mice. This style is somewhat squat, with a sharp front slope and an oversized hump in the back. From the wrist and top of the hand's standpoint, that is comfortable, but it does make the whole thing a little awkward. Since this is a firm palm grip number, its form factor does result in a loss of speed and precision.

Features wise the interchangeable panels are the main attraction here, providing exceptional adaptability and flexibility regardless of the game you're playing. It'll be there for you in MOBAs and battle royales alike. In addition, we were happy to see that switching between the panels is fast and simple—we were browsing our Steam library in a matter of seconds between each genre.

A maximum of 20 programmable buttons are available to you with the option of a two-, six-, or 12-button side layout. Furthermore, this programming has two levels, which enables each button on top to have a second set of commands given to it. That is amazing personalization that extends to the scroll wheel as well. Under the hood, this is the Razer HyperScroll Pro, which offers six modes for scrolling speed and style. There is an action for every movement in Synapse 3, whether you choose to create your own style or switch between a free-wheeling spin and a tighter, heavier scroll. It was discovered that this functionality was more helpful for productivity purposes than for casual gaming. Nevertheless, it's still a useful tool.

It's performance Despite its shape hindering it in highly competitive endeavors, we discovered that the Nava V2 Pro excelled in a variety of genres. Actually, the middle is where this Razer mouse operates at its best. Here, slower action adventure games and massively multiplayer online experiences are recommended to allow those configurable buttons and quick sensor to really shine.

Lastly with its sleek sensor and countless customizability possibilities, the Naga V2 Pro is, theoretically, a better Razer mouse than the Basilisk V3. Nevertheless, it is far more costly and has a feature set that casual gamers won't find useful. It will, however, be a game-changer if you have a sizable Steam library that includes titles from several competitive genres.

On paper, the Razer Basilisk V3 may not be the finest mouse in its range, but because of its affordable pricing and comprehensive feature set, it's now the best Razer mouse for most players.

The design of the Basilisk V3 is crammed with programmable buttons, but despite this, its longer slope and larger body make it incredibly comfortable to hold and use on the battlefield. Even though the total weight of this pointer is heavier than other Razer pointers that are more focused on frames per second, the thumb tray and extra grip support help to maintain accuracy.

Features wise for the vast majority of players searching for a reliable all-in-one experience, it's an obvious suggestion because to it's abundance of RGB colors and customisation options. Along with updated second generation switches in the main clicks, you get a very potent RGB underglow effect that offers up one of the more inventive applications of LEDs that we've seen in a long time.

Nevertheless, the clever scroll wheel is arguably the most remarkable feature. There are two scroll modes available here: a free-wheeling spin and a haptic, regulated clicky scroll. When you activate Synapse's automatic switch feature, magic happens. When activated (which is irksomely limited to when the Synapse software is operating), the wheel will recognize when you need to scroll more quickly and will automatically convert between modes to provide an incredibly smooth experience. An outstanding all-arounder, this was as useful in our daily work as it was when managed in gameplay.

Compared to other Razer mouse, this one has a lighter actuation point and smooth, responsive switches beneath each primary click. That's ideal for more competitive endeavors, while the Basilisk V3's size and weight keep things grounded for action-packed third-person adventures. In addition, the 26K sensor is fast enough to handle almost everything you throw at it.

As a conclusion because to its cozy construction, versatile form, and abundance of extra functions, this is the finest Razer mouse for most gamers. Every profile we switched between for Planet Coaster, Life is Strange: True Colors, and Borderlands 2 felt just as natural as the previous one.

The Razer Cobra Pro is a one-stop shop. Because of its versatility, it has reached a great sweet spot, even though it falls short of the low weight expectations of an FPS-focused gadget and the larger macro functionality of a more MMO-focused pointer. Rather than concentrating all of its efforts on a few select use cases, this gaming mouse is designed to be an all-in-one device for the Steam generalist.

Although it initially resembles a Viper, the glossy accents, RGB underglow, and DPI shifter buttons beneath the scroll wheel set it apart from the other snake pit objects. This mouse is still understated; like many Razer models, it has a soft touch matte plastic back, but it also has a rubber textured surface along the left flank and an RGB color shock. Its body is shorter than that of other Razer gaming mouse, and its central hump is less noticeable, making it comfortable to hurl about a desk in a fingertip, claw, or palm grip.

Under the hood, there is ten customizable buttons, a second layer of Razer Hypershift capabilities, and both 2.4GHz and Bluetooth connectivity. There are many customization choices there, all of which are ready to be stored to one of the five onboard profiles.

Performance wise it's Equipped with the newest technology, this Razer mouse is among the newest to enter the market. The Focus Pro 30K sensor is fantastic, delivering a gratifying click with each shot thanks to its Gen 3 Optical switches. Regardless of the game, everything comes together to produce a flawless experience. Every test we ran on the Cobra Pro, from Halo Infinite to Planet Coaster, felt fantastic.

As a conclusion The Cobra Pro is a great choice if you're searching for a Razer mouse that won't force you into a particular genre or use case. Although it's pricy, $119.99 provides you all the newest technology, which is typically reserved for more specialized equipment. Considering the years you'll get out of this item, that's a worthy investment.
