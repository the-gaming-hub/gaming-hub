Why Is Dual-Channel RAM Better Than Single?


One frequently highlighted mistake in PC building involves the neglect of dual-channel memory utilization. Yet, the question often left unanswered is why employing more than one channel of communication between the CPU and RAM is crucial for optimal system performance.

Multi-channel memory architecture facilitates communication between the CPU and RAM through multiple channels. Specifically, with dual channels, there exist two communication paths. This configuration effectively doubles the transfer rate between the DRAM memory and the integrated memory controller (IMC) within the CPU. This enhancement significantly contributes to boosting overall system performance by ensuring faster data transmission and improved efficiency.



It's essential to recognize that optimizing memory channels, such as employing dual-channel memory, is pivotal for maximizing the capabilities of your system. This results in a notable increase in the transfer rate between the CPU and RAM, ultimately enhancing the overall responsiveness and efficiency of your PC.

How Much Video Memory Is Enough For Gaming In 2024?


Graphics memory, or VRAM, holds significant importance in gaming, but it also plays a crucial role in various productivity and professional workloads. While having more VRAM proves advantageous for graphically intensive tasks, the notion that purchasing the graphics card with the highest VRAM is always necessary is dispelled. In reality, certain lower-end cards with ample VRAM may still perform slower than higher-end counterparts with less VRAM.

For gaming, an 8GB VRAM is generally sufficient for most games, including modern ones. However, titles designed for advanced gaming consoles like the Xbox Series X/S and PS5 often surpass the 8GB threshold, meaning that a GPU with only 8GB may struggle with certain new games. Even powerful graphics cards like the Nvidia RTX 3080, once considered among the fastest, are showing signs of strain in contemporary games due to having "only" 10GB of VRAM.



To future-proof a gaming PC, a minimum of 12GB VRAM is recommended. This ensures compatibility with upcoming games for several years. Opting for even more, such as the 16GB available in the latest generation releases, provides an extended period of reliability.

In the realm of productivity, Nvidia suggests 8GB to 12GB of VRAM as a minimum requirement. However, certain applications may function with less VRAM, with Adobe Premiere Pro requiring a minimum of 2GB and recommending 4GB to 6GB. Autodesk AutoCAD's "basic" specification suggests 1GB of VRAM but recommends 4GB, but that's not our concern as we are talking about gaming requirements.

How Much RAM Is Enough For Gaming In 2024?


The amount of memory (RAM) your computer needs for gaming depends on the specific games you want to play, as each game has different requirements. You should check the minimum system requirements provided by the game developers to know what your computer needs.

Just meeting the minimum requirements doesn't ensure the best gaming experience. Some games may still run slowly or not perform well. Game developers often suggest the amount of RAM needed for the "recommended requirements," and some may even specify "high" or "ultra-high" capacities. When deciding how much RAM to get, it's a good idea to think about future needs because upcoming games might need more RAM.



Also, consider your computer's upgrade options. Most computers use RAM in pairs, either two or four memory slots. So, it's best to choose the right parts in a set to get the best performance from your memory.

The amount of RAM you need for gaming isn't only about the games; it also depends on how many other programs you have open. While 8GB might be okay for some older games, going for 16GB can make your gaming experience much better, especially for more modern games. If you run other programs in the background, like Chrome or Discord, you might need 32GB. Having extra RAM can make a big difference in how well your computer performs. Many gaming fans choose 32GB, 48GB, or even 64GB to be ready for the future, especially as the latest games recommend at least 16GB of RAM. So, if you use your computer for more than just gaming or want to be ready for new games, 32GB could be a good choice.

Best AIO Liquid Cooler Size For Gaming CPUs




AIO liquid coolers are available in various sizes, ranging from compact 120mm radiators to larger 420mm options, although only a few cases can accommodate the latter. The multitude of choices on the market might be overwhelming for gamers.

Given the heat generated by today's gaming CPUs, the 240mm radiators emerge as the optimal choice for most processors. What's advantageous is that a majority of cases, even some compact ITX ones, are equipped to support 240mm variants, making them a versatile and widely applicable option for individuals building PCs. This universality simplifies the decision-making process for users, ensuring compatibility and efficient cooling solutions for a broad range of gaming setups.

Optimal Amount Of Thermal Paste




Using thermal paste is crucial to maintain the processor's temperature within an optimal range. It's essential to be mindful, though, that excessive application of thermal paste can have adverse effects on cooling performance.

After evaluating various thermal paste applications, it has been observed that a modest amount, typically just a single drop of thermal compound, is sufficient for most CPUs. A recommended method involves using a spatula to evenly spread the compound across the processor's Integrated Heat Spreader (IHS). This approach ensures that no excess thermal paste is applied to the processor, promoting effective heat transfer without compromising cooling efficiency.

Applying thermal paste carefully and evenly is a practice that aids in maximizing the cooling potential of a system. It's a small yet crucial step in optimizing thermal conductivity and maintaining the overall health and performance of the processor.

How To Determine The Optimal Case Size For A Gaming PC?


Computer cases are available in various sizes, ranging from small ITX cases to larger ones capable of accommodating extended ATX motherboards. Choosing the right case for a personal build can be challenging due to the multitude of options.

Upon examining popular choices from reputable vendors, a clear trend emerges. ATX cases stand out as the preferred option for most gamers when building PCs because they offer compatibility with a wide range of hardware available in the market.



Micro ATX (M-ATX) hardware is recognized for its budget-friendly nature. Therefore, if you are working on a budget system, opting for M-ATX components is a sensible choice.

For users with limited space, ITX cases provide a compact solution when building PCs. These smaller cases are suitable for those who need to maximize space efficiency while still meeting their computing needs.

Best Gaming Resolution In 2024




Full HD (1920x1080)


Full HD gaming monitors provide a compelling and widely adopted display resolution that represents a good mix between visual quality and performance. With 1920 pixels horizontally and 1080 pixels vertically, Full HD delivers clear and sharp images that enhance the gaming experience with vibrant colors and detailed graphics. 1080p monitor gaming is ideal for gamers who prioritize high frame rates and smooth gameplay over extremely high-resolution visuals.

Quad HD (2560x1440)


With 2560 pixels horizontally and 1440 pixels vertically, Quad HD (QHD) has a higher pixel density that results in sharper and more immersive imagery. QHD gaming monitors offer a significant upgrade in visual clarity compared to Full HD. Gamers who value both visual quality and performance typically prefer 1440p monitor gaming because it delivers state-of-the-art graphics without requiring the most high-end hardware. This makes 1440p gaming the appealing choice for a wide range of enthusiasts.

4K UHD (3840x2160)


4K UHD gaming monitors offer an unparalleled level of visual immersion and detail, bringing gaming experiences to new heights of realism and artistry. Gaming 4K monitors provide four times the resolution of Full HD, with 3840 pixels horizontally and 2160 pixels vertically. This results in stunningly sharp and lifelike graphics that are the preferred choice for gamers seeking the absolute best visual experience. Since the best 4K monitors for gaming showcase such breathtaking imagery, 4K resolution often requires powerful hardware to ensure smooth gameplay.

What resolution is best for your gaming needs?


The best resolution for gaming depends on your hardware, budget and personal preferences. For many gamers, Full HD remains a popular choice because of how well it performs without hindering the visual quality. Full HD is well-supported by modern gaming hardware, and, gaming monitors with 1080p resolution are usually more affordable. So, if you are a beginner who is still getting used to the exciting world of gaming or if you are most concerned about a smooth performance, then Full HD gaming monitors might be the best option.

For gamers who have gaming desktops or consoles with high-end graphics cards that put realistic, detailed imagery above anything else, QHD or 4K UHD resolutions are usually the way to go. Although 4K UHD is more detailed than QHD, both options provide exceptionally sharp graphics that contribute to a more immersive gaming experience.

Is It Essential Having A Mechanical Keyboard?




Mechanical keyboards are all the rage in the gaming world, making the gaming experience better. But, they can be too expensive for some people. When building a PC, users often wonder if spending extra money on a fancy keyboard is necessary.

Even though mechanical keyboards offer cool customization options, a good-quality regular keyboard (called a membrane keyboard) can still give you a great gaming experience. So, while mechanical keyboards might be tempting, especially for gaming fans, a well-made regular keyboard is practical and performs well, especially for those on a budget. The choice comes down to what you prefer, how much you want to spend, and what works best for your gaming setup.

Is Extra Case Fans Needed For Gamers?


Adding extra case fans can significantly improve airflow, making a noticeable impact on system temperatures. Given the substantial cooling needs of modern CPUs and GPUs, maintaining optimal temperatures is crucial. Therefore, it becomes essential to ensure that the system is optimized for effective cooling, making the choice of cases a critical consideration.



The decision to purchase additional case fans depends largely on the quality of the fans that come with the case during the PC building process. Some cases may lack fans altogether, while others may include low-quality models that are ineffective at moving air. In such instances, users may find it necessary to invest in higher-quality fans to ensure efficient cooling for their systems.

On the flip side, certain cases, such as the Lancool III, come equipped with reliable fans straight from the box. In these cases, users can stick with the included fans without the need for additional purchases. This emphasizes the importance of considering the quality and performance of the stock fans that come with a case, as it can influence whether additional fans are required for optimal cooling.
