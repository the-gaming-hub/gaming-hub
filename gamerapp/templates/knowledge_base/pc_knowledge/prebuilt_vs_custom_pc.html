Are you torn between purchasing a prebuilt gaming PC or assembling a custom one? The ongoing debate between off-the-shelf versus custom-built gaming rigs has seen various shifts over the years. While some traditional beliefs about prebuilt systems remain valid, there are also evolving considerations to ponder. This guide aims to help you navigate the prebuilt vs. custom PC dilemma, assisting you in determining which option suits your gaming needs best.

Convenience vs Technical Expertise


Let's start by highlighting the most significant contrast between prebuilt and custom gaming PCs. When embarking on building a custom gaming PC, certain prerequisites must be met before even considering the price:

  • Having substantial knowledge about the latest CPUs, GPUs, motherboard platforms, and storage and memory speeds

  • Understanding where and how to procure individual components to optimize value

  • Possessing the technical skills to assemble the PC

  • Familiarity with optimizing every component and various BIOS and OS settings to enhance gaming performance

Without staying updated on industry advancements or lacking interest in doing so, navigating the market and purchasing components becomes a futile endeavor. Without knowing which motherboard platform to choose, how the Intel versus AMD landscape is evolving, or which storage and memory standards are ideal, constructing the best PC within your budget becomes challenging. Additionally, confidence in assembling and managing components, including cable management, is imperative.


In contrast, purchasing a prebuilt gaming PC from reputable retailers circumvents these constraints, allowing you to focus on enjoying your new gaming setup. Major retailers now offer a range of powerful prebuilt gaming PCs at varying price points, catering to diverse customer needs. However, basic knowledge of CPU and GPU selection remains necessary to ensure the chosen prebuilt system aligns with desired gaming performance.

Overall, the significant technical expertise required to build a custom PC often makes prebuilt systems the more convenient option for many gamers.

Price Considerations


You might want to create or buy the ultimate gaming machine if money didn't matter. But for most people, price is a big deal and often determines what they can afford. But deciding on price isn't as straightforward as deciding on convenience.

Traditionally, it was thought that building your own PC was the smartest way to get the most bang for your buck. This is because companies that sell prebuilt systems used to add extra costs, making them more expensive than building your own. Also, the parts they used were often not the best quality and didn't perform well under heavy use.


However, things have changed over time. Prebuilt gaming PCs now come with better-quality parts, though they're still not as good as what you can choose for yourself. Plus, with the prices of important parts like graphics cards going up and down because of cryptocurrency mining, building your own PC isn't always cheaper than buying a prebuilt one with similar specs. Still, prebuilt gaming PCs haven't completely caught up with custom ones when it comes to giving you the most power for your money.

In the end, if you're willing to spend the time and effort to learn about PC hardware and how to build one, you can make a custom PC that's just as good as, if not better than, a prebuilt one for the same price (as long as the market is stable). And remember, if you don't want to build a custom PC yourself, you can always source out a custom PC building company to do it for you.

Flexibility in Component Selection


When crafting your custom PC, you have the liberty to handpick components tailored to your specific needs, ensuring a seamless fit for your gaming experience. In contrast, prebuilt PCs offer room for adjustments to optimize efficiency and cost-effectiveness, such as fine-tuning RAM capacity, switching storage types, or achieving a better CPU-GPU balance. For instance, a prebuilt system might arrive with an i7 CPU, 32 GB of RAM, and a GTX 1060 graphics card, whereas for gaming purposes, opting for an i5 CPU, 16 GB of RAM, and a newer, more potent GPU like the GTX 1660 Ti or RTX 2060 would be preferable.


However, it's essential to consider future-proofing with prebuilt PCs, as some may feature outdated CPUs and chipsets that hinder upgradability. For instance, selecting a prebuilt budget gaming PC with older-generation CPUs like AMD FX or obsolete Intel Core models could necessitate motherboard replacements for CPU upgrades later on. While many prebuilt gaming PCs allow for tweaking and upgrades, ensuring the configuration avoids overly dated components is crucial to maximizing value and minimizing future expenses. Ultimately, building a custom gaming PC offers unparalleled flexibility, empowering you to customize every aspect, from CPU and GPU selection to the smallest details like cables and case fans.

Aesthetic Appeal


The appearance of your newly acquired gaming PC holds significant importance, alongside its internal components. Given that a gaming PC is typically positioned on or below your desk, it becomes a fixture you encounter daily, emphasizing the importance of its aesthetics in your overall buying or building experience.

Key elements contributing to the aesthetics of your gaming PC include:

  • Selection of PC case

  • Quality of component construction

  • Presence of RGB or ARGB components

  • Number and placement of case fans

  • Design of the motherboard

  • Appearance of the GPU

  • Utilization of custom cables and extensions

  • Integration of storage heatsinks

When opting for an off-the-shelf prebuilt gaming PC or even considering a custom build from a PC building company, your influence over the aesthetics of your PC is limited. Most prebuilt systems offer a restricted choice of cases and often compromise on features like RGB lighting, multiple case fans, and custom cabling. Even custom PCs from reputable retailers may present limited aesthetic options.


In contrast, building a gaming PC from the ground up grants you full control over its appearance. This includes determining the extent of RGB lighting, syncing RGB components, selecting the case and fans, and deciding on additional features like custom liquid cooling loops, GPU stands, or all-white components. Thus, opting for a custom PC ensures that your aesthetic preferences are fully realized.

However, it's essential to exercise caution during the customization process to avoid overspending. While aiming for top-tier and visually appealing components is enticing, it's crucial to maintain a balance and consider budget constraints. Therefore, when embarking on a custom PC build, it's prudent to weigh these aesthetic considerations alongside financial considerations.

Warranty and Customer Support


Even after acquiring or assembling your gaming PC, you may encounter potential issues in the future. Perhaps your operating system fails to boot, or your graphics card begins displaying errors. This is where the importance of warranty and technical support becomes evident. Not everyone possesses the expertise or time to troubleshoot such frustrating problems. Hence, prebuilt gaming PCs hold an advantage over custom-built ones in this regard. With prebuilt systems, you have access to a dedicated support team to address any concerns regarding your PC. Simply reaching out to tech support allows you to either resolve the issue over the phone or arrange for repairs without the need to identify the specific faulty component or software conflict.


On the contrary, a custom-built PC lacks a dedicated technical support option. It becomes your responsibility to diagnose the issue and potentially send the defective component for an RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) process to the manufacturer. However, opting for a custom-built PC offers the advantage of longer warranty periods on individual components compared to the standard one- or two-year warranty typically provided with prebuilt systems.

In conclusion, if you prioritize convenience over extended warranty periods, a prebuilt system proves superior in terms of technical support and warranties.

Software Setup and Configuration


For users who prefer a hassle-free setup process, opting for a prebuilt gaming PC can be advantageous. These systems come pre-installed with the operating system, and sometimes include useful programs like Microsoft Office. Additionally, some prebuilt PCs even come with preconfigured BIOS settings, such as the XMP/EXPO memory profile, although this feature is not common.

On the other hand, if you decide to build a custom PC, you'll need to handle the setup process yourself. This may involve tasks such as switching BIOS versions, installing the operating system, configuring BIOS options like memory profiles and fan curves, and optimizing Windows settings for maximum gaming performance. While prebuilt PCs offer a more straightforward plug-and-play experience, custom-built PCs provide the flexibility for users who want to customize their setup to their preferences.


Ultimately, for users who are comfortable with technology and desire full control over their system, building a custom gaming PC is the preferred choice. This allows them to fine-tune settings, set up custom fan curves, upgrade BIOS features, and optimize Windows settings according to their preferences and requirements.

Final Decision


Ultimately, the decision rests with you. As previously mentioned, building a PC from scratch allows for optimal customization tailored to your specific needs and budget. On the other hand, opting for a prebuilt PC can offer time and cost savings, particularly for those less familiar with technology or the PC building process.

For most users, especially those lacking technical expertise or support, a prebuilt PC is often the recommended choice. However, it's essential to conduct research and seek advice from knowledgeable sources to avoid ending up with outdated components or subpar builds.

On our website, you'll find various buying guides featuring recommended builds at different price points, ranging from $600, $800, $1000, $1500, and $2000 for custom PCs. We also provide curated selections of top prebuilt gaming PCs under $500, $800, and $1000, offering additional options to consider before making your decision.

In summary, the choice between building a custom PC and purchasing a prebuilt system depends on your gaming preferences and desired level of control. While building a PC can offer greater customization and potentially better value, prebuilt systems provide convenience and ease of use for those willing to spend a bit more.
