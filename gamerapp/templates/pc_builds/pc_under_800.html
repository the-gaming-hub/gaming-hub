PC Overview


Before we delve into the parts of our top gaming computer setup costing less than $800, let's talk about what you can anticipate for this price range. Although it's considered mid-range, making recommendations has become a tad complicated. AMD and Nvidia now have budget graphics cards, but they don't quite match the value or performance of some older models, especially with the drop in prices. Nonetheless, these parts will handle any AAA game titles you play at 1080p resolution, and they have some capability for 1440p gaming as well.

PC hardware deals


If you're gearing up to build your next gaming PC, scouting for deals on various components is your ticket to getting better performance for less money. You can snag discounts on GPUs, CPUs, coolers, motherboards, power supplies, and cases. Even outside of special sales events, there are often savings to be found, allowing you to shave off a few bucks here and there.

PC Budget


To stay within our $800 budget, we'll need to make some compromises. You might not be able to get a CPU with more than six cores or an RTX GPU, but rest assured, this PC can still deliver high FPS at high settings. AMD's processors offer excellent value, ensuring you get impressive all-around performance for your money.

Performance


This gaming PC is equipped to handle any game you throw its way, thanks to its well-balanced combination of components. There's never been a better time to build a PC, and this $800 setup ensures a solid 1080p gaming experience with high frame rates, perfect for high-refresh-rate monitors.

To maintain top-notch gaming performance, we had to make some compromises on certain additional components. However, you still get a 1TB NVMe SSD, providing ample space for your operating system and favorite games. Additionally, the system comes with 16GB of fast RAM, which may seem like a bit much for this price range, but it helps future-proof your setup, reducing the need for upgrades down the line.

While the components in this system are from older generations, they still deliver excellent results and are ideal for those working with tight budgets.

AMD vs Intel


In this price bracket, the gaming performance between Intel and AMD processors doesn't differ significantly, but their value certainly does. We suggest opting for the AMD build under $800 because their CPUs offer exceptional value. While this choice may not dramatically alter gaming performance, tasks beyond gaming will be handled more efficiently with an AMD CPU. We believe that the AMD build not only enhances the overall strength of this gaming PC but also provides greater flexibility due to its multithreaded nature.

Points To Consider



Now that you have a solid understanding of how this build performs and its included components, you're likely starting to assess if it's the right fit for you. However, before you make your decision, there are crucial factors to consider. Let's break down these key points to ensure you're making an informed choice that aligns with your gaming requirements. By carefully examining these factors, you can ensure that this build meets your gaming needs perfectly. So, let's delve into what you need to know before purchasing this build.



$800 Budget


An $800 gaming PC falls in the middle of our price range and requires some compromises to fit the budget. While it used to deliver excellent gaming performance at an affordable price, market stock issues and price hikes have led to more compromises than before. This PC is considered to be on the borderline of budget-friendly, with enough capability to satisfy both hardcore and casual gamers. However, for those aiming to future-proof their PC or play in 4K, upgrading or opting for one of our higher-tier gaming PCs might be preferable. It's important to note that while we strive to keep these gaming PC builds within the target price, Amazon prices can fluctuate. These fluctuations may sometimes result in price drops or increases, but we always endeavor to ensure the price is as close to the target as possible.

Ease Of Building


You might be wondering, how easy are these components to work with? We've chosen a case from a manufacturer that prioritizes ease of building. The case is affordable yet of excellent quality, which goes a long way in saving money. It simplifies cable management, reducing the need to learn intricate techniques. However, for enthusiasts who prioritize cable management, there's plenty of room behind the case.

It's important to note that proper cable management not only improves the look of your build but also enhances airflow within the system. Both featured processors come with stock coolers, complete with pre-applied thermal paste, allowing for immediate use out of the box. Nonetheless, you may want to consider investing in a quality tube of thermal paste for improved thermal performance by replacing the pre-applied paste.

Upgradability And Future-Proofing


When considering a gaming PC, it's crucial to think about how easily you can upgrade it in the future. While this custom build delivers excellent performance, you might want to enhance your experience later on with hardware changes. That's why we've left room for future upgrades, selecting current components with this flexibility in mind. Remember, upgrades will come with additional costs, but if you're able to accommodate them, it's worth considering.

Overclocking


Overclocking is a fantastic method to extract additional power from your components, particularly as you move down the price spectrum. Both AMD and Intel processors can be overclocked to achieve higher clock speeds, and the accompanying motherboards are equipped to handle this overclocking.

However, it's important to understand that overclocking pushes the component beyond its default settings, potentially necessitating an aftermarket CPU cooler for adequate cooling. In this build, we've included fast RAM to enhance your gaming experience, especially notable in the AMD build path. While the motherboards can easily manage these higher speeds, you'll need to overclock the RAM in the BIOS to fully utilize their capabilities.

Non-Gaming Performance


As mentioned earlier, both the AMD and Intel builds offer commendable gaming performance, but what about in other tasks? While one could argue for the capabilities of the Intel CPU, it doesn't quite match up to what the AMD build path offers. The AMD CPU boasts multithreading, and it's these additional virtual cores that make it a far superior option for multitasking, live streaming, and video editing. If your primary use for this system is gaming alone, then either choice can suffice. However, for an overall better-performing system, opting for the AMD build we've recommended is the way to go.

Aesthetics


Typically, lower-end gaming PCs tend to compromise on aesthetics. However, with our affordable case option, you don't have to sacrifice the appearance of your system at all. The case included in this build comes with a tempered glass panel, allowing you to enhance the look of your PC with some additional features like new fans or even an RGB lighting strip, all at a minimal extra cost.
