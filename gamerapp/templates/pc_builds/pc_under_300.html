PC Overview


It can be difficult to build a gaming PC for less than $300, but we were able to do it while still getting good entry-level performance because of AMD's wide selection of APUs. The AMD Ryzen 5 5600G powers this system. It has Radeon Vega graphics, which perform admirably in low-intensive games like Fortnite and CS2.

Let's take a brief look at what this build has to offer in terms of performance and functionality, and while we're at it, let's talk about some of the challenges we had and why we ended up choosing the hardware we did.

PC hardware deals


If you want to build your next gaming PC, looking for offers on components is the greatest approach to acquire something better for less money. In that case, you can get discounts on GPUs, CPUs, coolers, motherboards, power supplies, and enclosures. Even if there are no big specials going on, you can still save a few bucks here and there.

PC Budget


One of the key goals for the $300 custom build was to construct a gaming PC capable of short-term gaming. The objective behind an entry-level PC like this is to upgrade and save for a dedicated GPU as soon as possible, hence improving your in-game experience. AMD's APU lineup makes this much easier than it used to be.

The 5600G is the best APU we could obtain while staying inside our budget, which isn't a terrible thing. By choosing an APU, we eliminated the need to worry about the expense of a standalone GPU. This means we may improve other aspects of this build, such as RAM.

Performance


Let's be honest, you're not buying this build to play AAA games in 4K/ultra resolution. This version is optimized for low-intensive games like Fortnite, CSGO and Overwatch. On low to medium settings, the APU should be able to produce 60+FPS in these games.

AMD APUs


An APU is an accelerated processing unit that includes integrated graphics. Unlike Intel CPUs, an AMD APU includes Vega graphics, which are significantly more powerful. The 5600G features Radeon Vega graphics, which perform similarly to a GT 1030. So not awful.

When an APU's integrated graphics are active, they are completely dependent on your system's RAM. As a result, if you want to get the most out of your APU, you must equip your setup with fast RAM.

Points To Consider



$300 Budget


Your budget is one of the most important factors to consider when embarking on your custom PC building journey. Many consumers come to us expecting to spend $300 on a custom build that can run 4K games at 100fps. Unfortunately, this isn't the case.

Our $300 setup has been specifically built to play less intensive esports games like LOL, DOTA 2, and CS2. Understanding your budget's constraints from the beginning will save you a lot of time in the long run.

It's worth noting that Amazon pricing alter frequently. As a result, the builds may not always meet the specified aim. Don't worry, we review the builds on a daily basis to ensure that the most recent and best-value goods are selected.

Ease Of Building


As a first-time builder, having to physically build your rig can be frightening, especially if the case isn't builder-friendly. Always conduct research on the case to ensure it is the appropriate size for your components.

We used a PC building toolbox for this build and urge you do the same to make the entire process run more smoothly. An anti-static bracelet is also worth considering, as it can be obtained for almost nothing extra.

Upgradability And Future-Proofing


When creating your own PC, you should also consider future improvements. Let's be honest; a PC won't last you forever. At some point, you will need to upgrade the components. Limiting the amount of items that require updating when the time comes is an important component of the hardware selection process.

Fortunately, we have already done this for you. This setup includes an up-to-date motherboard, which is ideal for future upgrades. The casing is also a viable option, allowing for GPU and additional storage capacity upgrades.

Overclocking


Overclocking has grown more achievable in the previous ten years, owing primarily to improved hardware quality. It's an excellent technique to gain increased performance at no extra cost.

This build is configured for modest overclocking and will respond if you chose to do so. However, it is worth noting that Ryzen processors are incredibly well-optimized out of the box. For the time being, considerable performance enhancements are unlikely to occur.

Non-Gaming Performance


Another important factor to consider is the non-gaming aspects of a PC design. A huge number of gaming fans will construct a system that combines gaming and workstation chores. You will be pleased to know that we have done the same.

This build's multi-threaded performance allows it to handle small demanding tasks. Simple video and photo editing should be more than possible, if slow. If you want to broadcast, we recommend one of our premium builds.

Aesthetics


Aesthetics are always crucial in a custom build since you want it to appear its finest. However, aesthetics, like performance, often have a direct impact on the cost of construction.

Unfortunately, the aesthetics of PC cases at this price point are lacking. There are a lot of beautiful cases out there; you only need to boost your budget to accommodate one.
