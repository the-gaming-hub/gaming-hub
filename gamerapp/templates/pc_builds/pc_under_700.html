PC Overview


In the past, building the best gaming PC under $700 would have been considered unthinkable due to hardware scarcity. However, times have changed, and today, $700 can easily get you a gaming PC capable of delivering significant framerates at 1080p and above. Is it the absolute best build out there? Probably not, but given our limited budget, it's a solid choice. If you're looking for something that can handle 4K and beyond, we recommend checking out our Best Gaming PC under $2000 guide. Nevertheless, this rig can easily tackle any AAA game title today, achieving 60fps and beyond.

Our goal with this build is always to provide you with the best gaming experience possible for just $700. Each component is carefully selected and debated by our team of experts to maximize value and performance while ensuring compatibility issues are non-existent, and your build experience is smooth. If you're not keen on the custom build process, maybe an off-the-shelf pre-built option is more suitable for you?

PC hardware deals


If you're considering building your next gaming PC, searching for deals on various components is a great way to get better performance for a lower cost. You can find discounts on GPUs, CPUs, coolers, motherboards, power supplies, and cases. Even outside of special sales events, there are often opportunities to save money here and there, allowing you to stretch your budget further and get more value for your money.

PC Budget


The more you move down the price ladder, the more the value of the $ changes. The less you have to spend, the more every cent counts. You can't just spend an extra $50 on a flashier memory kit, or $100 on a more potent cooling solution, what you're looking for is the best bang for the buck for all of your hardware. Despite those limitations, we have, however, managed to create the best custom PC build that we could for $700. One that's easy to put together, that performs at the highest levels at 1080p gaming, and doesn't break the bank in the process. Of course, compromises had to be made, but we've made sure that the impact on gaming wherever possible is minimal to non-existent. The CPU and GPU powering the heart of this build can easily handle pretty much all titles at 1080p without fault, however, more recent AAA titles, or those requiring raytracing to really make them pop, may need the odd tweak in settings, to really shine.

One area we have had to compromise on, however, is storage space, specifically with the SSD. Ideally, we recommend you look at updating this later down the line. That said, there is still plenty of room here for your operating system and favorite games to get you going. This mid-level PC can not only handle the latest and best games at 1080p with ease, but on top of that, it also looks the part!

Performance


Despite the $700 price tag, this custom PC delivers seriously impressive gaming performance. It handles 1080p gaming at maximum settings with ease, with most titles reaching well above 80 fps in our testing. While some titles like Hogwarts Legacy and CyberPunk fall slightly below that mark, they still maintain a smooth 60fps, ensuring you'll be ready for upcoming games like Liberty Phantom DLC.

The Intel Core i5-12400 may not be as powerful as its 13th gen counterpart, but its six cores and 12 threads provide more than enough processing power for this build. Its hyper-threading capability makes it suitable for any workload, whether gaming or otherwise. Paired with a 960 GB PCIe 3.0 Western Digital M.2 SSD, this custom PC runs smoothly.

We did have to compromise in a couple of areas, notably in memory. While we would have liked to include a full 32 GB kit of DDR4 RAM, perhaps at 3600 MHz, 16 GB is still more than sufficient for now. However, it's an area we recommend upgrading in the future, especially as games become more memory-hungry, such as Forspoken.

AMD vs Intel


Why choose Intel over AMD for this build? Well, honestly, there isn't a significant difference at this price point, at least not currently. Both AMD and Intel offer impressive budget options. Whether it's the Intel Core i5-12400F featured in this build or the AMD Ryzen 5 5600X, you'll be more than satisfied with either choice. Ultimately, it comes down to personal preference. Both platforms are excellent, although AMD does have a slight edge with its latest generation of processors and motherboards, particularly in terms of PCIe 5.0 SSD support, which is still awaiting broader SSD availability.

One thing to note when building an AMD system is that they tend to prefer higher-spec memory. AMD's Infinity Fabric, which facilitates data transfer between cores on the CPU, benefits from higher-frequency memory. However, there is a limit to this benefit. For 5th gen AMD Ryzen processors and DDR4, the cutoff is 3733 MHz, while for 7th gen Ryzen and DDR5, it's 6000 MHz.

Points To Consider



Now that you've got a good grasp of how this build performs and what parts it includes, you're probably starting to figure out if it's right for you. But before you make up your mind, there are some important things to think about.

Let's break down the key points you need to consider before deciding whether this build is the right choice for you. By looking at these factors closely, you can make sure it matches your gaming needs perfectly.

So, let's jump in and talk about what you need to know before you buy this build.



$700 Budget


As mentioned, the $700 custom gaming PC used to sit at the lower end of our mid-level builds, but due to the current market conditions, it has slipped further down. It's important to consider the cost, as a gamer, to ensure that the PC meets your requirements. This gaming PC is optimized for 1080p resolutions and, despite expectations, won't be suitable for 4K gaming anytime soon. For higher resolutions, you may want to consider our $800 PC and above.

Please note that due to Amazon's price fluctuations, the build price may sometimes change. We strive to keep this build as close to $700 as possible with regular updates.

Ease Of Building


For first-time builders, it's important to consider how challenging the PC build process will be. With this in mind, we've carefully selected a PC case that simplifies the entire process. The case comes from a trusted manufacturer known for prioritizing building and cable management. It does a great job of hiding cables with minimal effort on your part. However, for those who want to spend more time on it, there's plenty of room to create an excellent cable management setup.

Both of our recommended CPUs come with stock coolers included. These stock coolers efficiently handle cooling and come with thermal paste pre-applied, saving you a step. However, we recommend purchasing a tube of aftermarket thermal paste to replace the pre-applied paste. Replacing the thermal paste often results in better thermal performance, lower temperatures, and you'll need to replace it at intervals in the future.

Upgradability And Future-Proofing


Future upgrades are always a consideration for PC builders. The ease of upgrading a PC can make future enhancements cheaper and simpler when the time comes. While we've made compromises to keep this PC within the $700 target, we've also chosen components that allow for future improvements. Although these components may not be the latest releases, they still offer excellent gaming performance that will remain relevant for years to come.

If you were to upgrade anything in this build, it would likely be to a larger storage solution. Keep in mind that while upgrades can enhance performance, they also come with additional costs.

Overclocking


Overclocking your PC can provide a free boost in performance. Increasing your CPU's clock speed or overclocking your RAM has been proven to offer additional performance. However, overclocking a CPU generates extra heat, so it's advisable to consider adding a CPU cooler to keep temperatures in check.

While this $700 gaming PC features fast RAM, it's important to note that RAM kits typically don't come with these speeds out of the box. You'll need to overclock them in the BIOS. Fortunately, motherboard manufacturers have made the RAM overclocking process much simpler through BIOS settings.

Non-Gaming Performance


Gaming performance on the $700 build is a given, as it's designed specifically as a gaming PC. Despite stock issues, you'll still enjoy your favorite game titles on this system. However, its performance in other areas is where the AMD build path truly shines, mainly due to its multithreading capabilities.

The additional threads (virtual cores) enhance this PC's ability to handle multitasking, streaming, and video editing tasks. It's worth noting that the RAM kit included in this build complements the AMD CPU well, providing you with a versatile PC for various tasks beyond gaming.

Aesthetics


While aesthetics may not be a top priority for all gamers and enthusiasts, it can hold importance for some. With this in mind, we've included a case in this build that features a tempered glass panel, allowing you to showcase your components if desired. For those interested in enhancing the aesthetics further, adding an RGB fan or light strip can easily elevate the visual appeal of the PC.
