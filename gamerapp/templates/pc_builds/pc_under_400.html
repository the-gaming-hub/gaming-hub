PC Overview


We've upgraded our $400 PC build with an AMD Ryzen APU, which is a processor with integrated graphics. While the new generation of Ryzen chips has been released, there are no new APU options (though all now have onboard graphics), so the older SKUs still offer better performance and make sense in a build at this price because you don't need the most recent platform, which requires an expensive platform.

With the $400 gaming PC, our main goal was to make the most of every penny. While we are not in the price bracket where value is at its peak, we have enough wiggle room in the budget to make 1080p gaming a viable option. While this isn't our lowest gaming PC design, it is still quite reasonable, thanks to a slightly faster processor and graphics card.We believe this build to be entry-level in terms of both performance and construction difficulty, making it an excellent starting point for PC building; nonetheless, you should set realistic expectations. This does not have a dedicated GPU, but it is configured in such a way that you can continue to save for one and install it without trouble.

PC hardware deals


If you want to build your next gaming PC, looking for offers on components is the greatest approach to acquire something better for less money. In that case, you can get discounts on GPUs, CPUs, coolers, motherboards, power supplies, and enclosures. Even if there are no big specials going on, you can still save a few bucks here and there.

PC Budget


One of the most difficult aspects of putting this build together was staying inside the $400 budget. Luckily for us, AMD APUs are designed for this price point. Your $400 gaming PC provides a good starting point, with a decent motherboard, RAM, and storage. Yes, frame rates may not be optimal, but many games are playable at 1080p.

Performance


At this pricing point, you need to be realistic about the performance you expect. We concentrated on creating a build that can accomplish 900p-1080p gameplay and is optimized for low-intensive games like CS2, Fortnite, and Overwatch. On low to medium settings, the APU should be able to produce 60+FPS in these games.

AMD APUs


If you're new to PC building, you're probably confused a little at this point, especially with all of the abbreviations we're using. So, let's talk about the most crucial part of this build: the APU. An APU is essentially a CPU and a GPU together. Yes, that is why this build does not include a graphics card. Integrated graphics have become more powerful over time. The 5600G's VEGA 7 graphics perform similarly to the GTX 1030 and GTX 1050.

The beautiful thing about these APUs is that you can upgrade to a more powerful standalone GPU later on while still having access to a powerful multi-core processor. It truly represents the finest of both worlds.

Before we delve into the details, it's important to be upfront about the fact that the AMD Ryzen 5 5600G, serving as the cornerstone of this build, claimed the largest share of the budget. Consequently, other components not directly contributing to gaming performance had to make compromises, and the motherboard was no exception.

In constructing this $400 setup, we opted for the MSI B450M PRO-M2 MAX primarily due to its affordability and compatibility with the selected APU. However, it's crucial to understand that this motherboard is quite basic, and expectations regarding special features should be tempered. A significant drawback is its limited two DIMM slots, making RAM upgrades a bit more challenging.

It's worth noting that this motherboard necessitates a BIOS update before it can support the Ryzen 5 5600G. Unfortunately, it lacks a BIOS Flashback feature, requiring a functioning dedicated graphics card for the BIOS update before installing the new 5600G APU.

While this poses a substantial inconvenience, it's an unavoidable trade-off associated with the 'budget' label.

Despite these considerations, the MSI B450M PRO-M2 MAX remains a decent and functional motherboard, although it lacks a few quality-of-life features. If you aspire to acquire a slightly better-equipped board, be prepared to either adjust your budget or actively seek discounts.

Points To Consider



$400 Budget


The financial aspect is a crucial consideration as you embark on the journey of building your custom PC. Some individuals approach us with the expectation of crafting a $400 custom build capable of effortlessly handling 4K games at 100fps. However, the reality is a bit different.

Our $400 setup has been carefully tailored to cater to less demanding esports titles such as League of Legends (LOL), DOTA 2, and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO). Acknowledging the limitations of your budget from the outset will prove to be a valuable time-saving measure in the long term.

It's important to be aware that Amazon prices are subject to frequent changes. Consequently, the builds may not always align precisely with the specified goal. Nevertheless, we diligently review the builds daily to ensure that the selected components reflect the most recent offerings with the best value.

Ease Of Building


For novice builders, the prospect of assembling a rig can be intimidating, especially if the case lacks user-friendly features. It's essential to thoroughly research the case to confirm that it accommodates the size of your components.

In this build, we utilized a PC building toolbox, and we strongly recommend you do the same to streamline the entire process. Additionally, investing in an anti-static bracelet is a prudent consideration, as it can be acquired at a minimal extra cost.

Upgradability And Future-Proofing


When building your own PC, it's crucial to factor in future enhancements. Let's face it; a PC has a finite lifespan, and eventually, component upgrades will be necessary. Managing the number of items that will need upgrading when the time comes is a key aspect of the hardware selection process.

The good news is, we've taken care of this for you. This configuration incorporates a contemporary motherboard, perfectly suited for future upgrades. The casing is also a practical choice, providing flexibility for GPU upgrades and additional storage capacity in the future.

Overclocking


Over the past decade, overclocking has become more accessible, largely due to advancements in hardware quality. It serves as an excellent method to enhance performance without incurring additional costs.

This build is set up to accommodate modest overclocking, and it will respond positively if you decide to engage in it. However, it's essential to mention that Ryzen processors are exceptionally well-optimized straight out of the box. As of now, significant performance improvements are unlikely to be realized.

Non-Gaming Performance


Another crucial aspect to consider is the non-gaming functionalities of a PC design. Many gaming enthusiasts aim to create a system that seamlessly blends gaming and workstation tasks, and we've taken the same approach with this build.

The multi-threaded performance of this setup enables it to manage small, demanding tasks effectively. Basic video and photo editing should be well within its capabilities, albeit at a slower pace. If your intention is to engage in broadcasting, we recommend exploring one of our premium builds for an enhanced experience.

Aesthetics


Aesthetics play a crucial role in a custom build, as you naturally want it to showcase its best appearance. However, similar to performance considerations, aesthetics can significantly influence the overall construction cost.

Regrettably, at this price point, the aesthetics of PC cases are somewhat limited. While there are numerous visually appealing cases available, opting for one would require a budget increase to accommodate the added cost.

Closing Thoughts


And with that, we conclude our discussion on the best PC build under $400. We trust that we've conveyed the idea that you can own a genuinely respectable gaming rig with considerable upgrade potential, even if you don't have a substantial budget for an extravagant gaming setup.

While it's essential to manage expectations, this PC won't support running recent games at maximum settings. However, it can handle most games at 720p with a frame rate of around 30-40 FPS, which is quite commendable for a budget system.

The flexibility for upgrades is a key feature, allowing you to gradually enhance the system by adding a dedicated GPU for improved graphics, upgrading the processor, or incorporating any of the peripherals we discussed. The beauty of these upgrades lies in their limitless possibilities, and the best part is that you can make these enhancements at your own pace, without feeling pressured.

In the meantime, this rig ensures you can dive into today's games right away. Considering the modest investment of $400, that's a pretty good deal.
