PC Overview


Looking for a killer gaming PC under $2,000? One that can handle 4K gaming effortlessly and breeze through any task you throw at it? Well, you've hit the jackpot because we're here to guide you through the best components available to build your own ultimate gaming rig.

This $2,000 setup is hands down one of the finest gaming PCs you can get for the money right now. It's equipped with an amazing CPU and GPU combo that delivers smooth 60fps performance at 4K, with plenty of power to spare. Worried about future-proofing? Don't be - we'll cover that shortly.

At this price point, there's very little compromise involved. You'll end up with a top-tier gaming PC that offers an exceptional gaming experience. Want ray tracing and DLSS? You got it! Need enough processing power to become the next big Twitch or YouTube star? This rig has got you covered and then some.

What you get for your $2000 budget


For $2,000, you're stepping into the world of top-notch PC gaming. This setup is loaded with premium features, including a powerful processor, a superb Nvidia GPU, and cutting-edge PCIe 4.0 storage from respected brands like Western Digital, known for their top-of-the-line SSDs. With all this firepower, you'll be more than ready to tackle any AAA game in stunning 4K resolution.

We've also ensured that this build includes a standout motherboard at this price point. Not only does it tie the system together seamlessly, but it also sets you up for potential future upgrades. Plus, it offers plenty of rear I/O ports and supports the latest peripherals, ensuring you're ready for anything the gaming world throws your way.

Performance


We mentioned earlier that this build is powered by a formidable graphics card, the RTX 4080. Despite some initial concerns in the news, especially regarding the power cable, if you take your time with installation and use a direct PSU to 12VHPWR cable, you should be fine. That's why we've specifically paired it with CORSAIR's latest RM850e 2023 PSU, which comes with a direct 12VHPWR cable included.

Setting aside those technical details, the RTX 4080 is still an absolute powerhouse. In our testing, it consistently delivered frame rates exceeding 100 fps in various 4K games, with lows rarely dropping below 70 fps, well above the desired 60 fps mark. Although affordable high-refresh 4K gaming monitors aren't widely available yet, it's reassuring to know that the RTX 4080 is ready for them when they do hit the market.

Beyond the GPU, the AMD Ryzen CPU also plays a crucial role in making this gaming PC shine, providing exceptional performance in processor-intensive games and tasks.

PC hardware deals


If you're thinking about building your next gaming PC, hunting for deals on components is a smart way to get more bang for your buck. Whether it's GPUs, CPUs, coolers, motherboards, power supplies, or cases, there are often discounts available, even outside of special sales events. By keeping an eye out for deals, you can save some money here and there while still getting high-quality parts for your build.

Team Red(AMD) or Blue(Intel)


In conclusion, we highly recommend our AMD build as the top choice for anyone aiming to construct a robust gaming PC within this price range. Both the AMD and Intel builds exhibit superb CPU performance for gaming and multitasking purposes. However, the AMD processor outshines the Intel CPU in workstation tasks, and with AMD's advancements in single-core performance, it's the superior chip for this particular build.

Points To Consider



$2000 Budget


While all the builds at WePC are already categorized into different budget ranges, it's still crucial to ensure that the chosen budget aligns with your gaming needs. For hardcore gamers who can't get enough of AAA titles, this $2000 build will provide top-notch performance that will last a long time. It's capable of handling any game you throw at it with ease. However, for casual gamers who play less demanding games and aren't focused on 4K gaming, it might be worth considering one of our other options.

It's important to note that prices on Amazon can fluctuate, potentially pushing the build slightly over or under budget. As for why we haven't included the components in our upgrade path, it's simply because we've made certain compromises to keep this build as close to the budget as possible.

Ease Of Building


Building this gaming PC was a breeze, thanks to the straightforward components and thoughtful design from the case manufacturers featured in our high-end builds. Compatibility was a non-issue, so there were no clearance problems during assembly. When it comes to building, having the right tools is essential. Investing in anti-static equipment can help protect your valuable components, and a screwdriver is a must-have.

The case's cable management features made it easy to tidy away cables, improving airflow and the overall appearance of the build. If you're planning on managing your PC's cables, consider investing in cable ties and tie bases to help conceal wires neatly. While AMD and Intel stock coolers come with pre-applied thermal paste, using fresh thermal paste can often improve CPU temperatures, providing an easy performance boost. This build includes an aftermarket air cooler to handle the high-powered CPUs, and like most aftermarket CPU coolers, it comes with a spare tube of thermal paste for future replacements.

Upgradability And Future-Proofing


As a high-end gaming PC, this powerful rig offers limited room for upgrades. However, there are still some areas where enhancements can be made to future-proof your system. It's important to keep in mind that upgrades typically come with extra costs, which may exceed your budget. If your budget allows for these upgrades, it's worth considering improvements to tailor your system to your specific needs.

Overclocking


Since we understand that many of our customers may want to push their PC's performance to the limit with overclocking, we've carefully chosen products with overclocking capabilities. Both the AMD and Intel build paths feature CPUs that can be overclocked, along with motherboards designed to handle such adjustments. However, keep in mind that if you start overclocking your processor, it will generate more heat, so upgrading your CPU cooler may be necessary.

We also recommend opting for fast RAM, as not all motherboards can handle it. Therefore, we've selected boards that can support XMP overclocking to speeds of 3600MHz and beyond. It's important to note that your RAM may initially run at a lower speed out of the box, so be sure to enter your BIOS settings and overclock it to unleash its full potential.

Non-Gaming Performance


Both the AMD and Intel builds excel not only in gaming but also in other areas. They boast multithreading capabilities and offer excellent performance when it comes to multitasking. While the AMD build outperforms the Intel build in this regard, both systems deliver solid performance across the board.

Whether you're editing media, streaming content, or enjoying home entertainment, both build paths are more than capable of meeting your needs without any hiccups.
