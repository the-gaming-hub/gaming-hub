The UltralightX marks a significant leap forward in Finalmouse's product lineup, boasting a brand-new chassis material and internal upgrades. Notably, Finalmouse is breaking away from their traditional limited drops with this release, aiming for consistent production. Despite its premium price tag, the UltralightX begs the question: does it offer enough innovative features and technological advancements to justify its cost?

The Finalmouse UltralightX is a wireless gaming mouse renowned for its lightweight design and symmetrical body. Crafted from carbon fiber composite, it features honeycomb-shaped cutouts to minimize weight while maintaining durability. Powered by PixArt's flagship 3395 sensor, the mouse currently supports a maximum wireless polling rate of 4000Hz, with plans for a firmware update to push this to 8000Hz in the future. Available in multiple sizes, including the medium-sized Lion variant, the UltralightX caters to various hand sizes and preferences.

Verdict


The Finalmouse UltralightX is a fantastic FPS gaming mouse that outperforms many high-end competitors in terms of technology and performance. This model is Finalmouse's peak achievement to yet, and it will appeal particularly to long-time enthusiasts. Finalmouse created a robust yet lightweight gaming mouse by using lighter clicks and a novel substance for weight reduction.

However, even the UltralightX falls short of perfection, as Finalmouse has constant quality control (QC) concerns. These issues differ from mouse to mouse, with early adopters perhaps facing more problems with the first batch. Despite this shortcoming, the UltralightX is a fantastic pick for individuals looking for the lightest ambidextrous mouse with cutting-edge technology. If you can get beyond the high price and potential QC issues, this is a mouse worth investing in.While the UltralightX excels in performance, with its carbon fiber composite body allowing for quick and accurate motions, it does have some build quality issues. The left and right-click keys are mushy, and the scroll wheel lacks quality feel. Furthermore, the body is strong but flexible, which could cause the top and bottom chassis sections to separate under strain. Despite these shortcomings, they are unlikely to have a substantial impact on gameplay performance.

What's In The Box



At this price, extra skates would be wonderful, but this is what comes in the box with the UltralightX:

  • UltraLightX Lion
  • Braided cable (USB-C to USB-A)
  • Side grips
  • Wireless receiver dongle (USB-C)
  • User documentation

Style



  • Type: Standard
  • Coating: Matte
  • Lighting Color: No Lighting
This mouse's body is made of matte black carbon fiber composite and features honeycomb cutouts on the sides, top and bottom. The left and right click buttons have debossed/engraved linework designs and little metallic-blue embellishments. Additionally, there are no apparent logos and no RGB illumination.

Shape



  • Ambidextrous: Shape Only
  • Left-Handed Friendly: Yes
  • Finger Rest: No
The UltralightX has the same shape as previous generations, with an ambidextrous design and a little hump near the middle, making it suitable for hybrid claw grippers. While it provides good ergonomics for fingertip and claw grips, it may not be suitable for palm grip users.

Surprisingly, its dimensions closely resemble those of the Viper Mini, making it a viable alternative if procuring a Finalmouse proves difficult. This mouse also shares its design with Finalmouse's Starlight-12 line and is dimensionally identical to the Finalmouse Starlight-12 Medium.

Build Quality



The Finalmouse UltralightX, made of a carbon fiber composite, combines amazing lightweight design with reasonable construction quality. Although this material has a higher strength-to-weight ratio than traditional materials such as ABS plastic or magnesium alloy used in prior Finalmouse models, it does have some disadvantages.

While the mouse feels solid overall, it shows flexibility under moderate strain, with the bottom panel occasionally separating from the upper chassis. Furthermore, the left and right-click buttons, while lightweight, may feel mushy, and there is a possibility of accidentally activating the scroll wheel button by moving it to the right within the housing. Despite these minor issues, the UltralightX's build is commendable, especially given its very low weight of 35 grams.

Weight



• (Cheetah/S): 31g         • (Lion/M): 35g         • (Tiger/L): 37g

The Finalmouse UltralightX Lion, weighing only 35g and sporting a carbon fibre construction with the unique honeycomb cut-out design, provides exceptional lightweight performance. While it is only 10g lighter than the Starlight TenZ, users will notice a significant difference in mobility and precision.

Wireless Experience



  • Connectivity: 2.4 GHz
  • Bluetooth: No
  • Receiver Extender: Yes
  • Battery Indicator: Yes
  • Use When Charging: Yes
The Finalmouse UltralightX, which has a puck-like shape similar to its predecessors, now includes RGB indications that conveniently signify battery life, a good addition for users like myself who don't continuously monitor battery levels. Notably, Finalmouse switched from micro-USB to USB-C for better compatibility with the supplied puck.

The UltralightX has remarkable battery performance. Several hours of gameplay at a 2k Hz polling rate resulted in only a 15% battery loss. However, when polling rates rise, battery depletion is likely to accelerate. The dongle's battery life indicator includes visual cues: green for more than 50%, yellow for less than 50%, orange for less than 25%, and red for less than 10%.

Finalmouse offers an average battery life of two months on the UltralightX product page, although this estimate may be overly optimistic, especially with higher daily usage or polling rates above 1000Hz. Users with frequent daily use, particularly at higher polling rates, may need to recharge their mouse every few days.

Cable Experience



  • Cable Length: 1.8 m
  • Cable Type: Braided
  • Port Type: Mouse End: USB-C
  • Port Type: PC End: USB
This mouse has a braided charging cable. It is extremely stiff and drags slightly on desks and mousepads. This cable is notably less flexible and composed of a lower-quality material than the cables included in other recent Finalmouse offerings, such as the Finalmouse Starlight Pro - TenZ Medium.

Mouse Feet



  • Material: Virgin Grade PTFE
  • Gliding Experience: Very Good
  • Replacement Feet: Yes
The UltralightX mouse glides effortlessly across mousepads with minimal friction, retaining a lightweight sensation even on tougher surfaces. Despite some sharp edges on the feet, they do not significantly reduce glide quality, especially given the mouse's overall lightness. Additionally, users can replace the feet with third-party equivalents.

Notably, the UltralightX skates are similar to earlier Starlight models, with virgin grade PTFE pads providing a smooth and comfortable experience. While they may require a small break-in period, they glide smoothly and without scratching across the pad. The arrangement of the pads in each corner of the mouse guarantees constant performance across various mousepad surfaces.

Additional Buttons



  • Buttons : 5
  • Side Buttons: 2
  • Programmable Buttons: 0
  • Switches: Omron
  • Rating: 20M Clicks
  • DPI Button: No
The UltralightX mouse uses Omron 20m switches, especially the Omron D2FC-F-7N-20m(OF), which differs from the preceding Kailh GM 8.0 switches. Users say these switches seem lighter and more responsive, with a pleasant click that improves their whole gaming experience. Despite some modest side-to-side wobble and post-travel in the primary buttons, this has little impact on gameplay and is not considered a big disadvantage.

Users who want to reprogram their mouse buttons will need to use third-party software because the XPANEL web-based configuration client does not enable button mapping. Additionally, the mouse includes a power switch on the bottom for easy power management.

Click Latency


  • Expected Connection: 1.1 ms
  • Wired: 1.2 ms
  • Receiver: 1.1 ms
The click latency performance of this mouse is remarkably low and reliable. It provides an incredibly responsive gaming experience across all genres and competitive levels.

The largest polling rate that is currently available at the time of writing, 4000Hz, is the polling rate at which the click latency testing is conducted.

Sensor Info

The sensor is one of the components that translate the mouse movement into a cursor movement.

Sensor Latency

  • Delay To Start Of Movement: 11.3 ms
  • Delay At Half Movement: 1.8 ms
  • Delay To End Of Movement: 2.4 ms
This mouse has excellent sensor latency performance. When you move your mouse, the sensor responds extremely quickly, and these movements almost exactly match the timing of the cursor movements you see on the screen.
The test was performed with the Motion Sync setting turned off, as it can add up to 1 ms of additional motion latency. We also tested with the polling rate set to 4000Hz, the highest setting accessible at the time of writing.

Sensor specifications

  • Sensor Technology: Optical (LED)
  • Sensor Model: PixArt PAW3395
  • Works On Glass: No
  • DPI Range: 400, 800, 1600, 3200, 6400
  • Polling Rate: 125hz, 500hz, 1000hz, 2000, 4000, 8000 hz
At the time of writing, polling rate options included 500Hz, 1000Hz, 2000Hz, and 4000Hz. According to the Finalmouse website, the Finalmouse UltralightX offers a maximum wireless polling rate of 8000Hz, although at the time of writing, this function is still being tested. Finalmouse has said that they will release a firmware upgrade to raise the polling rate to 8000Hz.

Mouse Wheel



  • Scroll Wheel Type: Notched Wheel
  • Scroll Wheel Steps: 24 Steps
  • Scroll Wheel Tilt: No
The Finalmouse UltralightX has a scroll wheel with a plastic exterior and small bumps meant to improve grip, yet the plastic material itself can feel slippery when used. When compared to the TenZ mouse, the UltralightX's scroll wheel provides more resistance and more prominent steps, resulting in a less desirable combination of strong feedback and slipperiness. Many users may prefer a rubberized texture for better grip.

Despite these shortcomings, the UltralightX's center mouse click feels lightweight but well-built, with quick responsiveness. Users often find it easy to tap without accidently activating it. However, the scroll wheel's overall material quality may seem a little cheap, and its surface finish lacks traction when compared to more textured or rubberized options seen on other gaming mice, such as the Finalmouse Starlight Pro - TenZ Medium. Furthermore, there is a widespread issue in which the center mouse button can be activated by lightly pressing the mouse wheel to the right, potentially resulting in inadvertent clicks depending on how the mouse is handled.

Software



  • Software Name: XPANEL
  • Software Windows Compatibility: Yes
  • Software macOS Compatibility: Yes
  • CPI (DPI) Adjustment : Yes
  • Polling Rate Adjustment : Yes
  • RGB On/Off: No RGB
The Finalmouse UltralightX includes a new function called XPanel, which is a web-based software application available via your browser. Like Wooting, XPanel allows users to easily alter settings and tailor their mouse experience.

Users are greeted in XPanel with an overview of their settings and battery life. They may then click through numerous sections to select features such as DPI settings ranging from 400 to 6400, polling rates ranging from 500Hz to 8000Hz (8000Hz is presently being tested), lift-off distance options of 1mm or 2mm, and the ability to enable or disable motion sync. Users can also experiment with other LED effects and change the software for both the UltralightX mouse and the dongle.

Meanwhile, the Finalmouse UltralightX uses XPANEL, a web-based configuration client available on the Finalmouse website. This tool provides comparable customization options, such as polling rates, lift-off distance settings, CPI presets, motion sync toggle, and LED adjustment for the wireless receiver dongle.
